WFM · Detector Inspector03 / WFM Scope
What we do
Scope of Workforce Management
Click each pillar to learn more
Real-Time Management
Live floor monitoring, agent adherence tracking, and queue activity — happening every minute of your shift. WFM watches dashboards showing every agent's status in real time.

When something deviates — an agent goes unexpectedly offline, breaks run long, or queues spike — WFM acts immediately to rebalance coverage and protect the team's SLA. Marvin monitors phones; Joni monitors non-phones.

Reports & Monitoring
KPI tracking, absenteeism data, attendance reports, and performance dashboards shared with operations and leadership. Kurt manages all reporting functions.

All captured data feeds daily attendance logs, absenteeism rate calculations, KPI qualifier compliance, and payroll submissions. What gets tracked directly affects your performance record and compensation.

Staffing & Capacity
Scheduling, leave slot management, overtime deployment, and headcount forecasting. WFM builds and owns the schedule to ensure the right people are in the right place.

WFM approves leave within capacity limits, deploys overtime when volume demands it, and adjusts headcount plans when conditions change. Every schedule is built around maximizing coverage and protecting agent welfare.

Leave Policy

File at least 2 weeks in advance

This is a hard requirement — not a guideline. Last-minute leave requests cannot be accommodated.

2
Email your team leader

Include your reason, type of leave, and exact inclusive dates. Incomplete requests will not be processed.

3
WFM reviews and approves

Approval is based on available slots and capacity — it is not automatic. Plan early to improve approval chances.

4
Approved leave = Scheduled Absenteeism

Approved leaves do not negatively impact your unplanned absenteeism rate. File properly and it stays clean.

✓ Scheduled
Filed 2 weeks ahead, approved by WFM. Does NOT hurt your absenteeism rate.
✗ Unplanned
No advance filing or not approved. COUNTS against your absenteeism rate.

Overtime is extra hours beyond your regular shift — deployed by WFM when call volumes are high or coverage needs to be extended. OT is assigned, not self-declared. WFM communicates OT requirements ahead of time.

WFM · Detector Inspector10 / Schedule Adherence
Day-to-day expectation
Schedule Adherence

What is adherence?

  • Being on the right activity at the right scheduled time
  • Following your start time, break windows, and shift end exactly
  • Measured as % of shift time spent correctly on task

Why it matters

  • 1 agent off-schedule at peak can break the whole queue model
  • WFM staffing plans assume 100% schedule compliance
  • Adherence is monitored live and reported to leadership daily
Your daily shift routine — hover each step

Clock in on time & go queue-ready

Start tracker and be available on queue by your exact scheduled start — not 2 minutes after.

Take breaks within your assigned window

WFM assigns specific windows per your pattern. Notify if you need to adjust — do not self-reassign.

Stay on queue between breaks

Return immediately after each break. Unnecessary offline time flags on WFM's dashboard instantly.

Complete shift fully before clocking out

Finish all wrap-up. Never log out early without TL or WFM approval — undertime counts against your rate.

Adherence standard
100%
Expected of every agent
every shift, every day
Deviations should be the exception — always communicated in advance and always routed through the proper notification channel.
